Borrow a Life is an MVP built around a simple idea:
before judging a life from the outside, borrow a public window into it.

This is not a tour app, not a class marketplace, and not another mentor platform.
Borrow a Life lets people spend a short, safe, public session inside someone else's everyday rhythm.

On the home page, the core promise is clear:
Borrow a life. See your own differently.
You can preview real life windows in motion:
a software engineer in Berlin, a vegetable farmer, a line cook, and a street vendor.

On Explore, each life window is presented as a possible perspective:
who it is for, how long it lasts, the price, tags, and a safety score.
The user is not choosing a tourist activity.
They are choosing a life rhythm they want to understand.

Opening a listing, like Berlin Software Engineer,
shows why this life matters, who the host is,
what the public itinerary looks like,
what the user will experience,
and what is explicitly outside the boundary.

The booking flow is where the product becomes different.
Before a request can be sent, the user enters the start time,
duration, session mode, location type,
whether the host is verified,
whether private transport is involved,
and how close the nearest public exit point is.

The AI Safety Engine then analyzes the session.
It returns an allowed or rejected decision,
a safety score,
a risk level,
reasons,
warnings,
and rule violations.

If the session is safe, Borrow a Life generates a Boundary Contract.
It explains what is allowed,
what is not allowed,
and what rules both sides must follow
to keep the experience public, bounded, and respectful.

Borrow a Life is not about entering someone's private life.
It is about experiencing the public rhythm of their reality.

Most platforms help you book places.
Borrow a Life helps you borrow perspective.
